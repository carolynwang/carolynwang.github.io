A Brief Introduction

hey hi hello, i'm Carolyn. i'm a software engineer, photographer, and, if you couldn't tell from the way my website is set up, a bit all over the place.

here's a tl;dr about me:

  • chinese-american, grew up in northern virginia and am now based in the bay area (california)
  • graduated with a B.S. in electrical engineering and computer science and a minor in statistics from the universtiy of california, berkeley in december 2020
  • currently a software development engineer at amazon web services working on distributed data parallelism
  • rabbit zodiac sign, isfp myers-briggs, enneagram type 9>6>7, sagittarius and pisces rising, true neutral alignment, if you're into any that... though i personally have no idea what most of that means
  • got into photography after being the family vacation photographer for six years and finally decided to learn to use the manual settings during my senior year of high school
  • did a lot of drawing when i was young and recently started picking it back up during college

the slightly longer version:

after graduating [virtually] from uc berkeley, i started working as a software development engineer as amazon web services. i am on the sagemaker data parallel team, developing sagemaker's distributed data parallelism library for deep learning training jobs on sagemaker. prior to this, i had interned in one of the sister deep engines teams and had designed and implemented the initial release of sagemaker components for kubeflow pipelines. i also interned at mongodb for a summer, designing and implementing support for faceting for Atlas Search.

on the photography side, i started really getting into portrait photography during my senior year of high school. at that point, i had been the family photographer for many years (resulting in a lack of photos of me on vacations, but that's besides the point). i started discovering photography youtube channels, inspiring me learn to use the family dslr to its full potential. i then learned all about post-processing photos and have gradually been developing my editing style. what you see in my portfolio now is the result of five years of fiddling with the lightroom sliders, and it is still evolving. i mainly take photos of people, doing several grad photo sessions each grad season.

in addition to ~coding~ and photography, i also spend a good chunk of time drawing, cooking, baking, trying to play guitar, watching american television and korean and chinese dramas, and reading up on whatever topic i've taken an interest in at the time, which could be anything from finance to the allusions to christian theology in twenty one pilots music. and this is the exact reason why i say i'm all over the place. apparently, i'm incapable of choosing one thing and getting really good at it; i have a tendency to try to do anything i find interesting. i don't know if that's a good or a bad thing... perhaps it doesn't have to be either, it just is a thing.

anyway, that's all i have to say for now. if you want to chat about anything, just drop a message in my contact page!
